What Setback is

Setback generates an informational permitting roadmap — likely agencies, jurisdiction-specific flags, a realistic timeline, and a draft narrative — based on the project and location you describe. It is not legal advice, and it does not replace an official determination from the agencies with actual authority over your project.

No refunds, one exception

All sales are final. The one exception: if Setback's roadmap omits an agency or permit that was clearly required for your project type and jurisdiction, send proof — the rejection notice citing it, or a link to the jurisdiction's own code section — within 30 days of purchase, and that pull is refunded. This guarantee covers completeness of the roadmap only. It does not cover the timeline, the narrative's wording, or whether an agency ultimately approves your project — those are outside Setback's control.

Accuracy

Setback's output is generated by an AI model working from public permitting information and, over time, real outcomes reported by other users. Requirements change, vary by jurisdiction, and are sometimes applied differently than published rules suggest. Always confirm current requirements with the issuing agencies before submitting an application or beginning construction.

Limitation of liability

Setback's liability for any claim arising from use of the service is capped at the amount paid for the specific pull in question. Setback is not liable for delays, fines, stop-work orders, or other consequences arising from reliance on the roadmap without independent confirmation from the relevant agencies.

Reported outcomes

If you report an outcome (approved, approved with comments, or rejected) after using Setback, that report — along with your project's trade and general location — may be aggregated with other reports to improve roadmaps shown to other users. Individual reports are never published or resold with enough detail to identify the specific project or applicant.

Geography

Setback is built and tested against U.S. jurisdictions. Data quality outside the U.S. is not supported, and output for non-U.S. locations should be treated as unreliable.
