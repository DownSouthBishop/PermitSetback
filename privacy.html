Privacy Policy

Last updated: draft — not yet reviewed by an attorney

The short version: no account, no login, no name required to use Setback. Here's exactly what's collected and why.

What's collected

  • The project description and location you type in, sent to Setback's AI provider to generate your roadmap, and stored so future roadmaps for similar projects can improve over time.
  • Payment information, handled entirely by Stripe at checkout — Setback never sees or stores your card number.
  • An outcome report, if you choose to submit one — the approval status, tied to the same project and location.
  • Basic usage events (e.g., that a roadmap was viewed, that checkout started) used only to understand whether the product works, never sold or shared.

What's not collected

No account, no name, no persistent login. Voice input, if used, is processed by your browser's built-in speech recognition and is not sent to Setback as audio.

Third parties involved

  • Anthropic (and, as a fallback, Google) — process the project description and location to generate the roadmap.
  • Nominatim (OpenStreetMap) — checks that the location you entered is a real place, before anything else happens.
  • Stripe — handles payment. See Stripe's own privacy policy for how they handle card data.

How long data is kept

Project descriptions, locations, and outcome reports are retained to improve future roadmaps and to populate aggregate statistics shown on the site (e.g., "X% approved as drafted" for a given county). Aggregates never identify an individual project or applicant.

Your rights

Since no account exists, there's no login to delete — but if you'd like a specific submission removed, contact the address on the main site with enough detail (approximate date, location, project type) to identify it.
